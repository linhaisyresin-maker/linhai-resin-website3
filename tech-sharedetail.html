Standard Pretreatment of Ion Exchange Resins

Application Guide Linhai R&D Team

Proper pretreatment of ion exchange resins is crucial to ensure maximum exchange capacity and prevent contamination of your industrial system. New resins often contain residual manufacturing impurities that must be removed before service.

1. Initial Water Rinse

In the pretreatment device, maintain the liquid level about 10mm above the ion exchange resin layer to ensure there are no air bubbles within the resin bed. Rinse the resin multiple times with clean water until the effluent is completely colorless, odorless, and free of visual impurities. Filter the resin.

2. Chemical Conditioning

Alkaline Wash: Treat the resin with a 4% Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH) solution at a flow rate of 7ml/min for 30 minutes. Follow this with a large volume water rinse at 15ml/min for 20-30 minutes until the effluent pH approaches neutral.

Acid Wash: Treat the resin with a 4% Hydrochloric Acid (HCl) solution at a flow rate of 7ml/min for 30 minutes. Follow this with another large volume water rinse at 15ml/min for 20-30 minutes until the effluent pH is neutral.
